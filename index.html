CT Permit Guide
Source: portal.ct.gov/dmv
Step 1 — Before You Apply
Connecticut Work Zone Safety Course+
✓All new CT drivers must complete the Connecticut Work Zone Safety Course before applying for a learner's permit — this is the very first step in the licensing process
✓The course covers safe driving through highway work zones and is required by CT state law
✓Available online — free of charge
Students must complete this before their DMV permit appointment. Proof of completion is required when applying for the learner's permit.
Step 2 — Apply For Your Permit
Teens — Age 16–17+
✓Minimum age: 16 years old
✓Parent/guardian present at appointment, or notarized Form 2-D (Certificate of Parental Consent)
✓Bring completed Application for Non-Commercial Learner's Permit (Form R-229)
✓Pass vision test and 25-question knowledge test — need 20/25 to pass
✓Pay $40 testing fee + $19 learner's permit fee
Appointment required. Schedule & prepay online. Testing offices: Bridgeport, Cheshire, Danbury, Enfield, Hamden, Norwalk, Old Saybrook, Waterbury, Wethersfield, Willimantic.
Adults — Age 18++
✓Apply for an Adult Instruction Permit at the DMV
✓Pass vision test + 25-question knowledge test (20/25) — pay $40 testing fee + $19 permit fee
✓Must hold permit at least 90 days before road test
Permit Restrictions — Age 16–17
Rules While On A Permit+
PassengersParent, legal guardian, or qualified trainer only
Cell phonesNo use including hands-free
Qualified Trainer: Must be 20+, licensed 4+ years, no suspensions in past 4 years.

Call Scripts
Students On The Autism Spectrum (ASD)+

Use this script when a parent calls about driving lessons for a student on the autism spectrum. Focus on empathy, safety screening, and matching the right instructor.

Step 1 — Opening
Say This

"Absolutely — thank you for sharing that. Our instructors work with many different learning styles and comfort levels."

Step 2 — Screening Questions
Say This

"Before we schedule, can I confirm a couple quick things: does your student have their learner's permit, and are they generally able to stay comfortable and follow the instructor's directions for a full lesson? And are there any safety concerns or past experiences you'd like us to know about?"

Goal: Confirm permit status, lesson readiness, and safety considerations before booking.
Decision Pathways
A — Student Is Ready (Permit + Comfortable + No Safety Concerns)

Ask about driving experience: "Have they driven at all in a parking lot or quiet neighborhood?"

No experience yet: "No problem — we can begin with a calm first lesson focused on comfort, basics, and step-by-step coaching."
Some experience: "We'll start from where they are and build confidence from there."
Next Steps
✓Proceed with scheduling, add notes to student account, inform instructor
B — Permit Yes, But Student Gets Anxious Or Overwhelmed

"Are there any situations that tend to feel overwhelming — like heavy traffic, highways, busy intersections, or lots of directions at once?"

"We can start at a manageable pace and build up as they gain confidence."

Next Steps
✓Proceed with scheduling, add notes, inform instructor
D — Safety Concerns / Extreme Reactions (Escalation Required)

Examples: panic attacks, aggression, refusing to stay in car, inability to follow directions

"To make sure we support your student in the safest way possible, I'm going to take down your information and give you a call back. I want to ensure we match them with the right instructor."

Next Steps
!Create a Teams post in Scheduling with parent's details and key concerns
!Tag RM to advise which instructor to schedule with
!Add notes if student already has an account
E — Student Tried Lessons Before And It Went Badly (Escalation Required)

Pinpoint what happened: "What part was most challenging — the instructor's coaching style, anxiety during the lesson, or certain driving situations like traffic or turning?"

"We can absolutely use that to make a better plan and help them restart with a more comfortable approach."

Next Steps
!Create Teams post in Scheduling with what happened previously, tag RM
Universal Closing (Any Branch)

"Thank you for sharing all of that. Based on what you told me, the best next step is to take down your information and give you a call back. I want to ensure we match them with the right instructor."

Support Guidelines
Customer Language & Company Representation+

Always speak as the company — use first-person plural at all times.

❌ Avoid
"They will get back to you."
"They don't allow that."
"They're working on it."
✓ Use Instead
"We can help you with that."
"We are reviewing your request."
"Our team will follow up."
Third-person may only be used when clearly referring to a separate entity — e.g. "The state agency sets that requirement." When in doubt, default to "we."
Key Pointers For Effective Interactions+
1Stick to what the customer needs to know. Avoid over-explaining internal processes — share only what impacts their next steps.
2Don't read internal notes out loud. Notes contain jargon. Summarize the relevant outcome in plain terms instead.
3Avoid thinking out loud. Silence while navigating is fine. Use short transitions: "Give me a quick moment while I pull that up."
4Avoid announcing leadership decisions. Don't say "my manager said…" — present it as a unified team action: "We're taking care of it for you."
5Be purposeful with questions. Ask only what's immediately useful. "Can you confirm the email you used to register?" beats "Did you register online or in person?"
6Trim call length. Resolve and close as efficiently as possible. If the issue is solved, wrap up politely and confidently.
Script Improvements
Instead Of…
Try This
"I'm just going to pull your account, check the notes, and see if there's a payment there. One moment."
"Let me quickly check your account — one moment."
"Our manager said she's reviewing it and will maybe follow up tomorrow if she can get to it."
"We've flagged this for follow-up and will update you as soon as possible."
"Hmm, let me see… okay, so there's a note here from a few days ago…"
No need to narrate — silently review and summarize relevant info if needed.
Handling Escalations+
1Stay calm & professional. Remain composed — customers may be frustrated with the policy, not with you.
2Use empathy first. "I completely understand that this is frustrating, and I appreciate you reaching out to discuss it." Let them express frustration before responding.
3Restate the policy with confidence. "Our policy requires at least 24 hours' notice for cancellations, which is why the fee has been applied." Apologize for the situation, not the policy.
4Offer solutions within your authority. Options may include: forfeit the lesson instead of paying the fee, reschedule, or confirm future reminders are set up.
5Avoid immediate manager escalations. De-escalate first: "Our leadership team follows the same policies I've shared, but I want to make sure we explore all possible solutions first." Offer a callback instead of an immediate transfer.
6If escalation is inevitable, gather: customer name & contact info, lesson date & reason for dispute, and previous conversations. Then: "I will submit your request for review, and a manager will get back to you within two business days."
7Close on a positive note. Summarize next steps and thank the customer: "I truly appreciate you reaching out. Please let us know if there's anything else we can do to support you."
Late Arrivals & Early Departures+

When a student arrives late or leaves early, present both options clearly and document their choice.

Option 1
Pay Fee & Make Up Time
Pay the partial lesson fee ($50) to reschedule and make up the missed time.
Option 2
Forfeit Time, No Fee
Waive the fee and accept the reduced lesson — missed time will not be recovered.
For minors or students working toward a certificate, forfeiting time may impact testing eligibility. Make sure they understand this before deciding.
Example — Susie Arrived 30 Minutes Late

Susie was scheduled for a 120-minute lesson but only completed 90 minutes.

→Option 1: Pay $50 to reschedule the 30 minutes she missed.
→Option 2: Forfeit the 30 minutes — no additional charge, no make-up.
Always document the student's choice. If unsure how to handle a situation, escalate to your Squad Leader.
