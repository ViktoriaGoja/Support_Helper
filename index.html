CT Permit Guide
Source: portal.ct.gov/dmv
Eligibility & How To Apply
Teens — Age 16–17+
✓Minimum age: 16 years old
✓Parent or legal guardian present at appointment, or notarized Certificate of Parental Consent (Form 2-D)
✓Bring completed Application for Non-Commercial Learner's Permit (Form R-229)
✓Pass vision test (bring glasses/contacts if needed) and 25-question knowledge test — need 20/25 to pass
✓Pay $40 testing fee + $19 learner's permit fee
Appointment required. Schedule & prepay online at CT DMV. Testing offices: Bridgeport, Cheshire, Danbury, Enfield, Hamden, Norwalk, Old Saybrook, Waterbury, Wethersfield, Willimantic.
Adults — Age 18++
✓All new drivers must get a permit first — even if you just turned 18
✓Apply for an Adult Instruction Permit at the DMV
✓Pass vision test + 25-question knowledge test (need 20/25) — pay $40 testing fee + $19 permit fee
✓Must hold permit at least 90 days before road test — no parental consent needed
Knowledge Test
Test Format & Tips+
Questions25 multiple choice
Passing score20 correct (80%)
SchedulingAppointment only — schedule & prepay online
Fail vision testCannot proceed — must reschedule
Study the CT Driver's Manual. Download the free CT DMV mobile app for practice questions.
Permit Restrictions — Age 16–17
Rules While On A Permit+
PassengersParent, legal guardian, or qualified trainer only — no other passengers
CurfewNo curfew — but must always have qualified trainer present
Seat beltsAll passengers must use permanently installed seat belts
Cell phonesNo cell phone or mobile devices — including hands-free
Qualified Trainer: Must be 20+, licensed 4+ years, no suspensions in past 4 years. Can be parent, grandparent, foster parent, or legal guardian.

Training Paths — Teens Age 16–17
Commercial / Secondary School — 120-Day Path+
✓30 hours classroom training (including safe driving practices + 2-hour parent training)
✓40 hours practice driving behind the wheel
✓Hold permit at least 120 days before road test
The TNS 30-Hour Full Driver Education course satisfies this classroom requirement.
Home Training With Qualified Trainer — 180-Day Path+
✓22 hours classroom-type home training
✓Plus 8-hour safe driving practices course at a commercial/secondary school (including 2-hour parent training)
✓40 hours practice driving + hold permit at least 180 days
The TNS 8-Hour Safe Driving Course satisfies the commercial school component of this path.
Adults — Age 18+ (90-Day Path)+
✓Hold adult instruction permit at least 90 days
✓Complete an 8-hour safe driving practices course
✓Pass the road test
Road Test
What To Bring+
✓Physical learner's permit — you will not be tested without it
✓Completed Form R-229 — signed by Qualified Trainer
✓EDEC Driver Education Certificate — sent to DMV by TNS after course completion
After passing, wait 48 hours before visiting DMV with Road Test Evaluation Report.
If Failed+
✗Wait at least 14 days before retaking
✗Repay the $40 testing fee for each attempt
License Restrictions — Age 16–17
Restrictions After Getting Your License+
Passengers — First 6 monthsParents, legal guardian, or qualified trainer only
Passengers — Second 6 monthsImmediate family members only
CurfewNo driving 11 PM – 5 AM until age 18
Cell phonesNo use (including hands-free) until age 18
Curfew exception for school/religious activities sanctioned by the school — must travel directly to/from event only.

MA Permit Guide
Source: mass.gov RMV
Eligibility
Who Can Apply+
✓Must be at least 16 years old and a Massachusetts resident
✓Must not hold a driver's license or learner's permit in any other state
✓Must be clear of outstanding obligations to any state
✓If under 18: written parental or legal guardian consent required
Permit valid for 2 years. If expired, must reapply and retake the knowledge exam.
How To Apply
Application Process+
✓Start application online at myRMV (mass.gov) — required before visiting a Service Center
✓Schedule appointment at an RMV Service Center (or AAA if a member) to verify identity documents
✓Pass a vision screening and pay $30 application fee
✓Take knowledge exam at Service Center or online at home after documents are verified
Knowledge Exam
Exam Format & Requirements+
Questions25 questions
Passing score18 correct to pass
Time limit25 minutes
FormatAt RMV Service Center or online
LanguagesAvailable in 37+ languages
Online exam: computer only (no phones/tablets), working camera required, no other people in room. Must complete within 60 days of application.
Permit Restrictions
Rules While Driving On A Permit+
Supervising driverLicensed, age 21+, at least 1 year experience, seated beside driver
Curfew (under 18)No driving midnight–5 AM unless parent or legal guardian (licensed, 1+ year) is supervising
Out-of-state drivingCannot drive in another state if it violates that state's law
Always carryMust have learner's permit when driving
MA License Guide
Source: mass.gov RMV
Driver's Education — Under 18
Required Driver's Ed Program+
✓Required for all applicants under age 18
✓30 hours classroom instruction
✓18 hours on-road: minimum 12 hours BTW + minimum 6 hours observation
The TNS Full Driver Ed Bundle + 12-Hour BTW & 6-Hour Observation Bundle satisfies all three state components.
Junior Operator License (JOL) — Ages 16½ To 18
JOL Requirements+
✓Hold valid permit for at least 6 consecutive months with a clean record (any suspension resets the clock)
✓Complete approved driver's ed program (30 hours classroom + 12 hours BTW + 6 hours observation)
✓Complete at least 40 additional supervised hours certified by parent/guardian (reduced to 30 hours with a driver skills development program)
✓Parent or guardian participates in 2 hours of driver's ed (waived if completed within past 5 years)
✓Pass a final exam
JOL Restrictions
Restrictions On A Junior Operator License+
Passengers — First 6 monthsNo passengers under 18 (except immediate family) unless licensed adult 21+ is present
CurfewNo driving 12:30 AM – 5:00 AM unless licensed adult 21+ is present
Cell phonesNo cell phone use while driving
Passenger restriction liftsAt age 17
Nighttime restriction liftsAt age 18
Full unrestricted licenseAutomatically at age 18 — no action needed
Adult License — 18+
Getting A License As An Adult+
✓Must still obtain a learner's permit first — no driver's ed or permit hold period required
✓Complete at least one 2-hour driving lesson with TNS before testing with TNS
✓Pay RMV fees in advance via myRMV at mass.gov
✓Upon passing, signed permit becomes temporary license starting the next day; official license mailed to address on road test application
